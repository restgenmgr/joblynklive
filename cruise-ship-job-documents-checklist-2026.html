Cruise Ship Jobs · Document Checklist 2026

Documents Needed for
Cruise Ship Jobs in India 2026:
Complete Checklist for Freshers

JobLynk.live · Updated May 2026 · 8 min read · For Freshers Without Experience

One of the most common reasons Indian freshers get rejected for cruise ship jobs is not lack of skills — it's incomplete or incorrect documentation. This complete 2026 checklist tells you every document you need, how to get it, how long it takes, and exactly what it costs.

Getting a cruise ship job as an Indian fresher in 2026 requires a specific set of documents issued or recognized by the Directorate General of Shipping (DG Shipping), the International Maritime Organization (IMO), and individual cruise lines. The process can seem overwhelming at first, but when broken into steps it is entirely manageable — even for someone with zero sea experience.

1Mandatory Documents — Every Cruise Ship Job

These are non-negotiable. No cruise line will process your application without every item on this list.

  • ✓
    Valid Indian PassportMinimum 2 years validity remaining at time of application. Must have at least 3–4 blank visa pages.₹1,500 – ₹3,500 (fresh/tatkal)
  • ✓
    Indos Number (Indian National Database of Seafarers)Free online registration via DG Shipping portal (dgshipping.gov.in). Required before applying for CDC or any maritime certificate.Free
  • ✓
    CDC — Continuous Discharge CertificateIssued by DG Shipping. This is your official seafarer's identity book. Required by all cruise lines operating internationally. Apply via Mercantile Marine Department (MMD) office in Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, or Kochi.₹1,000 – ₹1,500
  • ✓
    STCW Basic Safety Training (BST) CertificateFour-module safety course from a DG Shipping approved Maritime Training Institute. Valid for 5 years. Mandatory for all seafarers under international law.₹12,000 – ₹22,000
  • ✓
    Medical Fitness Certificate (ENG1 or equivalent)Issued by a DG Shipping approved doctor. Tests vision, hearing, cardiovascular fitness, and general health. Valid for 2 years.₹2,500 – ₹6,000
  • ✓
    Yellow Fever Vaccination CertificateRequired for vessels calling at ports in Africa and South America. Get vaccinated at a government-approved Yellow Fever vaccination centre.₹300 – ₹600
  • ✓
    Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)Issued by your local police station or via the Passport Seva Portal. Confirms no criminal record. Required by all cruise lines.₹500 – ₹1,200
📌 Total Cost Estimate for Mandatory Documents (Freshers)

Passport (if not held): ₹3,500 · Indos: Free · CDC: ₹1,500 · STCW BST: ₹18,000 · Medical: ₹4,000 · Yellow Fever: ₹500 · PCC: ₹800 — Total: Approx ₹28,300

2Additional Documents for Hotel / Hospitality Department

If you are applying for a housekeeping, F&B, reception, or any other hotel department role on a cruise ship, you also need these certificates in addition to the basic STCW package:

  • ✓
    Crowd Management CertificateRequired under STCW for all passenger ship hotel staff. Covers passenger safety management, emergency muster, and crowd control. 1-day course.₹3,000 – ₹5,500
  • ✓
    Crisis Management & Human Behaviour (CMHB)Required for supervisory and senior hospitality roles. Covers leadership under emergency, crowd psychology, and passenger management. 2-day course.₹5,000 – ₹9,000
  • ✓
    Security Awareness CertificateBasic maritime security awareness training — required for all crew under ISPS Code. Often included in STCW BST packages.₹1,500 – ₹3,000 (if separate)

3Educational & Professional Documents

  • ✓
    Educational Certificates (Class 10, 12, Degree)Attested photocopies of all marksheets and passing certificates. Some cruise lines require degree certificates for supervisory roles.Attestation: ₹200 – ₹500
  • ✓
    Previous Employment Letters / Experience CertificatesIf you have prior hotel or hospitality experience (even land-based), include reference letters and experience certificates. This significantly improves your chances.Free from employer
  • ✓
    Updated CV / Resume (Maritime Format)A CV formatted to cruise line standards — typically 1–2 pages with photo, objective, experience, and certifications clearly listed.Free (or professional help: ₹500–₹1,500)
  • ✓
    Passport-Size PhotographsRecent photos with white background — minimum 6 copies. Some cruise lines require specific dimensions.₹100 – ₹200

4Visa & Travel Documents (Pre-Joining)

Once you receive a job offer, the following are required before you can physically join your ship:

  • ✓
    US C1/D Transit Visa (for US-based cruise lines)Required to join ships in US ports (Miami, Port Canaveral, etc.) or transit through the US. Apply at the US Embassy in Mumbai or Chennai. Processing: 4–8 weeks.USD 185 (approx ₹15,500)
  • ✓
    Schengen Visa (for European cruise lines)Required for ships operating in European waters. Apply at the relevant European embassy based on your first port of entry.€80 (approx ₹7,200)
  • ✓
    Joining Letter from Cruise Line / Manning AgencyOfficial offer letter confirming your position, joining date, vessel name, and salary. Required for visa applications.Provided by employer
⏱️ How Long Does the Full Process Take?

For a fresher starting from scratch in Mumbai — Indos registration (1 week) + STCW course (1 week) + CDC application (3–4 weeks) + Medical (1 day) + PCC (1–2 weeks) + US Visa (4–8 weeks) = Total: 2–4 months from start to job-ready. Start early!

5Step-by-Step Timeline for Mumbai Freshers

1
Week 1 — Register Indos NumberVisit dgshipping.gov.in and complete online Indos registration. Free and done in 1–2 days.
2
Week 1–2 — Get Medical CertificateVisit a DG Shipping approved doctor in Mumbai for ENG1 fitness certificate. Book in advance.
3
Week 2–3 — Complete STCW BST CourseEnroll in a DG Shipping approved MTI in Mumbai for the 5–7 day Basic Safety Training package.
4
Week 3–4 — Apply for CDCSubmit CDC application at MMD Mumbai with all documents. Processing takes 2–4 weeks.
5
Week 3–4 — Apply for Police ClearanceSubmit PCC application at your local police station or via Passport Seva Portal.
6
Week 4–5 — Get Yellow Fever VaccinationVisit a government-approved vaccination centre. Certificate issued same day.
7
Month 2–3 — Apply for US C1/D VisaBook US Embassy appointment in Mumbai (early — slots fill up). Bring all seafarer documents.
8
Month 3–4 — Start Applying to Cruise LinesSubmit applications to cruise line recruitment agencies with your complete document set.
